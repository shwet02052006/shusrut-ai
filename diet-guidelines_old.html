Basic Diet Guidelines

Essential nutrition principles for optimal health and wellbeing

Essential Macronutrients

Carbohydrates (45-65% of calories)

Good sources: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes

Avoid: Refined sugars, white bread, sugary drinks

Benefits: Primary energy source, brain function, fiber for digestion

Proteins (10-35% of calories)

Good sources: Lean meats, fish, eggs, beans, nuts, dairy

Daily need: 0.8-1g per kg body weight

Benefits: Muscle maintenance, immune function, tissue repair

Healthy Fats (20-35% of calories)

Good sources: Olive oil, avocados, nuts, fatty fish

Limit: Saturated fats, avoid trans fats

Benefits: Heart health, brain function, vitamin absorption

Daily Food Group Recommendations

Vegetables (2-3 cups daily)

Variety: Dark leafy greens, colorful vegetables

Examples: Spinach, broccoli, carrots, bell peppers

Benefits: Vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, fiber

Fruits (1.5-2 cups daily)

Best choices: Fresh, frozen, or dried without added sugar

Examples: Berries, citrus fruits, apples, bananas

Benefits: Vitamin C, potassium, natural antioxidants

Whole Grains (6-8 servings daily)

Examples: Brown rice, quinoa, whole wheat bread, oats

Serving size: 1 slice bread, 1/2 cup cooked grains

Benefits: Sustained energy, B vitamins, fiber

Dairy/Alternatives (2-3 servings daily)

Options: Low-fat milk, yogurt, cheese, fortified plant milks

Benefits: Calcium, protein, vitamin D

Hydration Guidelines

Water Intake

Men: About 15.5 cups (3.7 liters) daily

Women: About 11.5 cups (2.7 liters) daily

Include: Water from food and all beverages

Best Beverages

Primary: Plain water, herbal teas

Moderate: Coffee, unsweetened tea

Limit: Sugary drinks, alcohol, high-caffeine drinks

Meal Planning & Timing

Meal Frequency

Recommendation: 3 balanced meals + 1-2 healthy snacks

Timing: Every 3-4 hours to maintain energy

Portion control: Use smaller plates, eat slowly

Healthy Plate Method

1/2 plate: Non-starchy vegetables and fruits

1/4 plate: Lean protein

1/4 plate: Whole grains or starchy vegetables

Smart Snacking

Good options: Nuts, fruits, yogurt, vegetables with hummus

Avoid: Processed snacks, sugary treats, high-sodium foods

Special Considerations

Age-Specific Needs

Children: More calcium and protein for growth

Adults 50+: More vitamin B12, calcium, vitamin D

Pregnancy: Extra folate, iron, calcium

Medical Conditions

Diabetes: Monitor carbohydrates, choose complex carbs

Heart disease: Limit sodium, saturated fats

High blood pressure: DASH diet principles

Food Allergies & Intolerances

Common: Gluten, dairy, nuts, shellfish

Management: Read labels, find alternatives

Consultation: Work with dietitian if needed

Foods to Limit

High Sodium Foods

Limit to: Less than 2,300mg daily

Avoid: Processed meats, canned soups, fast food

Alternatives: Fresh herbs, spices, lemon juice

Added Sugars

Limit to: Less than 10% of daily calories

Hidden sources: Sauces, yogurt, cereals

Alternatives: Fresh fruit, unsweetened options

Processed Foods

Minimize: Ultra-processed foods, fast food

Read labels: Choose foods with fewer ingredients

Focus on: Whole, minimally processed foods

Practical Tips for Success

Meal Prep Strategies

  • Plan weekly menus in advance
  • Batch cook grains and proteins
  • Pre-cut vegetables for easy access
  • Keep healthy snacks readily available

Shopping Smart

  • Shop the perimeter of the store first
  • Make a list and stick to it
  • Don't shop when hungry
  • Read nutrition labels carefully

Building Healthy Habits

  • Start with small, sustainable changes
  • Listen to hunger and fullness cues
  • Practice mindful eating
  • Allow occasional treats in moderation
