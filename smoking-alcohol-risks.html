Risks of Smoking & Alcohol

Understanding health risks and finding paths to healthier living

Smoking Health Risks

Cancer Risks

Primary: Lung cancer (85% of cases linked to smoking)

Other cancers: Throat, mouth, esophagus, bladder, kidney, pancreas

Risk reduction: Starts immediately after quitting

Cardiovascular Disease

Increased risk: Heart attack, stroke, peripheral artery disease

Impact: Damages blood vessels, increases clot formation

Timeline: Heart attack risk drops 50% within 1 year of quitting

Respiratory Problems

Conditions: COPD, chronic bronchitis, emphysema

Symptoms: Shortness of breath, persistent cough, reduced lung function

Progression: Damage is progressive but quitting slows progression

Other Health Effects

Immune system: Increased infections, slower healing

Reproductive health: Fertility issues, pregnancy complications

Appearance: Premature aging, dental problems, skin damage

Secondhand Smoke Risks

Adults Exposed to Secondhand Smoke

Heart disease: 25-30% increased risk

Lung cancer: 20-30% increased risk

Stroke risk: Increased by 20-30%

Children's Health Risks

Respiratory: Asthma, bronchitis, pneumonia

SIDS: Increased risk of Sudden Infant Death Syndrome

Development: Slower lung growth, more ear infections

Alcohol Health Risks

Short-term Risks

Immediate: Impaired judgment, accidents, alcohol poisoning

Risky behaviors: Unprotected sex, violence, drunk driving

Health effects: Dehydration, hangover, digestive issues

Long-term Health Problems

Liver disease: Fatty liver, hepatitis, cirrhosis

Cancer risks: Breast, liver, throat, esophagus, colorectal

Heart problems: High blood pressure, cardiomyopathy

Mental Health Impact

Depression: Alcohol is a depressant affecting mood

Anxiety: Can worsen anxiety disorders

Addiction: Risk of alcohol use disorder

Safe Drinking Guidelines

Men: No more than 2 drinks per day

Women: No more than 1 drink per day

One drink: 12oz beer, 5oz wine, 1.5oz spirits

Benefits of Quitting Smoking

Immediate Benefits (Within hours/days)

20 minutes: Heart rate and blood pressure drop

12 hours: Carbon monoxide levels normalize

2 weeks: Improved circulation and lung function

Long-term Benefits

1 year: Heart disease risk cut in half

5 years: Stroke risk reduced to non-smoker levels

10 years: Lung cancer risk cut in half

Cessation Strategies

Nicotine replacement: Patches, gum, lozenges

Medications: Prescription options available

Behavioral support: Counseling, support groups

Reducing Alcohol Consumption

Strategies to Cut Back

Set limits: Decide how many drinks per week

Alcohol-free days: Have several days per week without alcohol

Smaller portions: Use smaller glasses, dilute drinks

Alternative Activities

Social: Meet friends for coffee instead of drinks

Stress relief: Exercise, meditation, hobbies

Rewards: Find non-alcohol ways to celebrate

When to Seek Help

Warning signs: Can't control drinking, hiding consumption

Health problems: Physical or mental health declining

Professional help: Counseling, support groups, medical care

Support Resources

Smoking Cessation Resources

National Quitline: 1-800-QUIT-NOW (1-800-784-8669)

Apps: QuitNow, Smoke Free, QuitGuide

Website: smokefree.gov

Alcohol Support Resources

SAMHSA Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357)

AA Meetings: Alcoholics Anonymous meetings

Apps: Sober Grid, I Am Sober, EasyQuit Drinking

Healthcare Provider Support

Primary care: Discuss with your doctor

Specialists: Addiction medicine specialists

Mental health: Counselors and therapists

Tips for Success

Quitting Smoking

  • Set a quit date and tell others
  • Remove all smoking materials from home
  • Identify and avoid triggers
  • Reward yourself for milestones

Reducing Alcohol

  • Keep a drinking diary
  • Find new ways to socialize
  • Practice saying "no" to drinks
  • Focus on health benefits of cutting back

Building Support

  • Tell friends and family about your goals
  • Join support groups or online communities
  • Work with healthcare professionals
  • Be patient with yourself during the process
Important Notice: Quitting smoking or reducing alcohol consumption can be challenging and may require professional support. Always consult with healthcare providers for personalized cessation plans and medical supervision when needed.
