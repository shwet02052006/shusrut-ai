Essential Health Tests & Screenings

Stay proactive with regular health screenings and understand important medical tests

Routine Blood Tests

Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Frequency: Annually

What it checks: Red blood cells, white blood cells, platelets, hemoglobin

Why important: Detects anemia, infections, blood disorders

Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)

Frequency: Annually

What it checks: Glucose, electrolytes, kidney function

Why important: Monitors diabetes, kidney health, electrolyte balance

Lipid Panel

Frequency: Every 5 years (more often if high risk)

What it checks: Cholesterol levels, triglycerides

Why important: Assesses heart disease risk

Heart Health Tests

Blood Pressure Check

Frequency: At least every 2 years

Normal range: Less than 120/80 mmHg

Why important: Prevents heart disease, stroke, kidney disease

Electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG)

Frequency: As recommended by doctor

What it checks: Heart rhythm and electrical activity

Why important: Detects heart rhythm problems, heart attacks

Stress Test

Frequency: When symptoms present or high risk

What it checks: Heart function during physical activity

Why important: Evaluates coronary artery disease

Cancer Screenings

Mammography (Women)

Age to start: 40-50 years

Frequency: Every 1-2 years

Purpose: Breast cancer screening

Pap Smear (Women)

Age to start: 21 years

Frequency: Every 3 years (21-65)

Purpose: Cervical cancer screening

Colonoscopy

Age to start: 45-50 years

Frequency: Every 10 years

Purpose: Colorectal cancer screening

Prostate Screening (Men)

Age to start: 50 years (earlier if high risk)

Tests: PSA blood test, digital rectal exam

Purpose: Prostate cancer screening

Diabetes & Metabolic Tests

Fasting Blood Glucose

Frequency: Every 3 years (starting at 45)

Normal range: Less than 100 mg/dL

Purpose: Diabetes screening

HbA1c Test

Frequency: Every 3 years (or as directed)

Normal range: Less than 5.7%

Purpose: Average blood sugar over 2-3 months

Thyroid Function Test

Frequency: Every 5 years (starting at 35)

Tests: TSH, T3, T4

Purpose: Thyroid disorders screening

Bone & Joint Health

Bone Density Test (DEXA)

Age to start: 65 (women), 70 (men)

Frequency: Every 2 years

Purpose: Osteoporosis screening

Vitamin D Test

Frequency: As recommended

Normal range: 30-50 ng/mL

Purpose: Bone health, immune function

Eye & Vision Tests

Comprehensive Eye Exam

Frequency: Every 2 years (annually after 60)

What it checks: Vision, glaucoma, cataracts, macular degeneration

Glaucoma Screening

Age to start: 40 years

Frequency: Every 2-4 years

Purpose: Early detection of glaucoma

Important Reminders

Prepare for Tests

  • Follow fasting instructions if required
  • Bring your medication list
  • Inform about any symptoms
  • Ask questions about results

Risk-Based Screening

  • Family history may require earlier/more frequent testing
  • Lifestyle factors affect screening recommendations
  • Discuss personal risk factors with your doctor
  • Some tests may be covered by insurance

Emergency Signs

  • Chest pain or shortness of breath
  • Sudden severe headache
  • Vision changes or loss
  • Unexplained weight loss
Important Notice: Health test recommendations may vary based on individual risk factors, family history, and existing health conditions. Always consult with qualified healthcare providers for personalized screening schedules and medical advice.
